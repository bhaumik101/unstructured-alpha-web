How Signal Scores Are Calculated

Each ticker's Confluence Score (0–100) is built from a set of macro signals that are statistically correlated with that ticker's future price moves. Signals include FRED macroeconomic data (yield curve, PMI, jobless claims), EIA energy data, SEC insider transactions, short interest from FINRA, and alternative signals like Congressional trades and options flow.

Signals are scored individually (Bullish/Bearish/Neutral), lag-validated against historical price data, and then combined using statistical weights. A score above 60 = Bullish, below 40 = Bearish, 40–60 = Neutral. Score history is recorded daily for each viewed ticker.
