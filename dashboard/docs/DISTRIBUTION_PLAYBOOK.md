# Unstructured Alpha Distribution Playbook

## Objective

Reach the first 100 external activated users without expanding the core product
surface. An activated user has searched at least one ticker and added at least one
ticker to a watchlist or portfolio.

Traffic alone is not the goal. The primary funnel is:

1. Visit a focused research or use-case page.
2. Open a ticker or the free application.
3. Search a ticker.
4. Add a holding or watchlist ticker.
5. Return within seven days.
6. Start a Pro trial after experiencing a meaningful change or alert.

## Positioning

Primary promise:

> Know when the macro backdrop around the stocks you own materially changes.

Supporting proof:

- 47 macro, credit, energy, volatility, sentiment, and market signals.
- Data from public and primary sources.
- Ticker-specific context rather than generic macro commentary.
- Explicit evidence limits, sample sizes, timestamps, and invalidation conditions.

Avoid leading with the number of features. Lead with a concrete portfolio decision
or change the investor could otherwise miss.

## Distribution portfolio

### 1. Search acquisition

Build pages only where Unstructured Alpha has enough evidence to provide a useful
answer. Do not index empty or generic ticker states.

Priority page families:

- Focused investor pages: uranium/nuclear, semiconductors, regional banks,
  energy producers, and rate-sensitive growth stocks.
- Signal explainers: what changed, why it matters, affected sectors, history,
  source, freshness, and limitations.
- Weekly research pages: dated, evidence-led market changes with links to
  relevant signals and tickers.
- Comparison pages: the macro backdrop across a small peer group, without
  pretending the score is a price target.

Every indexed page should have one clear next action: check a ticker free.

### 2. Founder-led community distribution

Publish useful analysis directly where concentrated investors already gather.

Initial communities:

- Uranium and nuclear investors.
- Energy and commodity investors.
- Semiconductor and AI infrastructure investors.
- Regional-bank and credit-cycle investors.
- Finance clubs, investing societies, and small investment communities.

Post structure:

1. State the market question.
2. Show the signal change and source.
3. Explain which tickers or sectors are exposed.
4. Include the limitation or invalidation condition.
5. Link to the relevant research page, not the generic homepage.

Do not lead with a product announcement. Lead with a finding.

### 3. Owned audience

Create a free weekly email that remains useful without a paid subscription:

- Three meaningful macro changes.
- Which sectors or common holdings are most exposed.
- One chart or compact table.
- One reason the interpretation may be wrong.
- Links to the underlying signal pages and a free ticker check.

The email should generate habitual return traffic. Pro should monetize continuous
monitoring, personalization, history, and alerts rather than hiding basic insight.

### 4. Partnerships

Target small publishers and communities before large influencers:

- Substack writers in uranium, energy, macro, credit, and sector investing.
- Finance professors and investing clubs.
- Independent research communities.
- Discord and Slack group administrators.
- Podcast and newsletter operators needing data-backed weekly segments.

Offer a co-branded weekly data section, embeddable chart, or tracked research link.
Avoid paid sponsorships until activation and retention are measured reliably.

## Landing-page wedge sequence

Do not launch every page simultaneously. Ship and measure one wedge at a time.

1. `/uranium`
2. `/semiconductors`
3. `/regional-banks`
4. `/energy`
5. `/rate-sensitive-stocks`

A wedge earns expansion only when it produces one of:

- 25 qualified visitors and at least 10% application click-through.
- Five activated users.
- Two meaningful external backlinks.
- One repeatable community or publisher relationship.

## Metrics

Measure by campaign and landing page:

- Unique qualified visitors.
- Application click-through rate.
- Account creation rate.
- First ticker search rate.
- First watchlist or portfolio addition rate.
- Day-1 and day-7 return rate.
- Trial starts.
- Paid conversions.
- Revenue per activated user.
- Source-level retention, not just source-level traffic.

North-star metric for the current stage:

> Weekly active external users with at least one saved ticker.

## First 30 days

### Week 1

- Index `/uranium`.
- Publish three uranium-focused research posts.
- Contact ten small uranium or nuclear publishers/community operators.
- Record every link with consistent UTM parameters.
- Review activation manually for every new user.

### Week 2

- Publish the first weekly macro-change email.
- Create a semiconductor acquisition page only if uranium tracking works.
- Add internal links from relevant signal and ticker pages.
- Remove or noindex empty ticker pages.

### Week 3

- Run five founder-led demos for finance clubs or investing groups.
- Offer one co-branded research section to a small newsletter.
- Interview every activated user available.
- Fix onboarding friction before adding another product feature.

### Week 4

- Compare wedges by activation and return rate.
- Double down on the best community and page family.
- Pause channels producing clicks without saved tickers.
- Publish a transparent month-one evidence report.

## Guardrails

- Never advertise unvalidated accuracy as established predictive performance.
- Never present the Confluence Score as a price target.
- Display data freshness and source.
- Do not index pages with no useful evidence.
- Do not buy broad paid traffic before organic visitors activate and return.
- Do not add more signals merely to create marketing claims.
